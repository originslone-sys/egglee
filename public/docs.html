📊 Understanding Hatch Chance

The "Hatch Chance" column shows the probability that a newly hatched chick will become this species. When an egg hatches successfully, the species is randomly selected using these weights: 70% Common, 25% Premium, 5% Rare. This means incubating eggs is the only way to potentially get a Rare or Premium chicken without buying one directly.

🥚 Egg Production

Eggs are your primary source of income. Understanding how production works helps you maximize earnings.

Production Cycle

The game runs a production cycle every 10 minutes, totaling 144 cycles per day. During each cycle, every alive and fed chicken has a chance to produce an egg.

probability_per_cycle = eggs_per_day / 144

For example, a Common chicken (1 egg/day) has a 0.69% chance per cycle to produce an egg. Over 144 cycles, this averages out to ~1 egg per day. Production is probabilistic, so daily output may vary slightly.

Selling Eggs

Click "Collect Eggs" to sell all available eggs to the system at the fixed price of $0.12 per egg. The USDT is instantly added to your wallet balance.

Buying Eggs

You can also buy eggs directly from the system for $0.20 per egg via MetaMask (USDT/BEP-20). Purchased eggs can be incubated just like produced eggs, with the same 30% hatch success rate and species probabilities.

💡 Why Buy Eggs?

Buying eggs to incubate is a strategy to rapidly expand your flock. Although you pay $0.20 per egg (vs. $0.12 selling value), a successful hatch gives you a chicken worth $1-$20. With a 30% success rate and weighted species chances, the expected value makes it a worthwhile investment for growth.

🌾 Feed System

Feed is the fuel that keeps your farm running. Without it, your chickens stop producing and will die within 72 hours.

How Feed Works

Every production cycle (every 10 minutes), the system calculates the total feed needed by all your alive chickens:

feed_per_cycle = SUM(each_chicken.feed_per_day) / 144

If you have enough feed, it's automatically deducted and your chickens produce normally. If not, all your chickens enter starvation mode — they stop producing eggs and a 72-hour death timer begins.

🔥 Incubation & Hatching

Incubation lets you turn eggs into new chickens — the only way to grow your flock without buying directly. Both produced and purchased eggs can be incubated.

The Incubation Process

🥚 Egg (produced or bought) → ⏳ Incubate 72h (0.5 feed/day) → 🎲 Hatch Roll (30% success) → 🐣 Chick!

What Eggs Can Be Incubated?

All eggs — whether produced by your chickens or purchased from the system — can be incubated. They all have the same 30% success rate and the same species probabilities.

Expected Value Analysis

The expected value of incubating one produced egg (opportunity cost = $0.12 sell price):

EV chicken value = 0.70 × $1.00 + 0.25 × $10.00 + 0.05 × $20.00 = $4.20
EV = 0.30 × $4.20 - $0.12 (egg) - $0.15 (incub. feed) = $1.26 - $0.27 = +$0.99

The expected value of incubating one purchased egg ($0.20 cost):

EV = 0.30 × $4.20 - $0.20 (egg) - $0.15 (incub. feed) = $1.26 - $0.35 = +$0.91

Both scenarios have strong positive expected value, making incubation a worthwhile strategy long-term despite the 70% failure rate per attempt. Note: this EV does not include the chick growth feed cost ($0.60), which brings net EV to approximately +$0.39 (produced) or +$0.31 (purchased) per attempt.

💀 Death Mechanics

Chickens can die in two ways. Both are permanent — dead chickens cannot be revived.

Natural Death (Lifespan)

Every chicken has a fixed lifespan determined by its species. When the lifespan expires, the chicken dies automatically at the next production cycle.

SpeciesLifespanTotal Eggs (theoretical)Gross Revenue
Common183 days~183 eggs~$21.96
Premium221 days~663 eggs~$79.56
Rare269 days~1,345 eggs~$161.40

Starvation Death

If your feed balance runs out:

  1. All your chickens immediately stop producing eggs
  2. A starvation timer starts for each unfed chicken
  3. If you refill feed before the deadline, starvation is cleared and production resumes
  4. If a chicken goes 72 hours (3 days) without feed, it dies permanently
Critical: Starvation affects ALL your chickens at once. If you run out of feed with 20 chickens, all 20 start starving simultaneously. Refilling feed within 72 hours saves them all — failing to do so kills them all.
🌾 Feed runs out → ⚠️ Starvation starts (no eggs) → ⏰ 72h countdown → 💀 Death (permanent)

Deposits

To deposit USDT into Egglee:

  1. Copy the platform's deposit wallet address from the Wallet tab
  2. Send USDT (BEP-20 only) from your MetaMask
